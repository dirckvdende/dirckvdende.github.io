Puzzle #2

Suppose we have a collection of ants we know are all related to each other in some way. In our fictional universe, an ant shares exactly half of its genes with its parent (we will also pretend ants have one parent). Concretely this means that if the parent has some ordered list of genes, then each of its children has a list of genes that matches the parent genes at exactly half of the positions. For example, ATGGCG can be a parent of ATCTAG, but not of TACGGG.

Place the ants in the family trees such that every ant is placed directly below an ant that shares exactly half of its genes.
